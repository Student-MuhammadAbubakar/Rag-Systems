Answer: I understand your need for clarity on the situations in which you can request a refund. Let me guide you through it:

1. **Product/Service Defect:** If the product or service you received has a defect or doesn't match the description, you are typically eligible for a refund.
2. **Cancellation within Grace Period:** If you change your mind and decide to cancel your order within the specified grace period, you can usually receive a refund.
3. **Unauthorized Charges:** If you see charges on your account that you didn't authorize, we will investigate and assist you in resolving the issue and obtaining a refund.
4. **Event Cancellation:** If you purchased tickets for an event that gets canceled and no alternative date is provided, you should be eligible for a refund.
5. **Duplicate Charges:** If you notice multiple charges for the same item, please notify us immediately, and we will rectify the situation and issue a refund.
6. **Non-receipt of Goods:** If you haven't received your ordered goods within the expected timeframe, you may qualify for a refund.

It's important to note that refund policies can vary depending on the vendor or service provider. I recommend reviewing the specific terms and conditions or contacting the vendor directly for a comprehensive understanding of their refund policy.
